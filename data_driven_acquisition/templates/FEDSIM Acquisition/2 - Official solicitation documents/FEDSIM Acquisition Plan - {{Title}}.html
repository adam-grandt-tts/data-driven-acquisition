Lessons Learned and Best Practices

  1. The FAR requires all sections to be addressed. If a section is not applicable, do not put not applicable; instead, write a brief sentence explaining why it is not applicable. Template language for "not applicable" situations for some sections is provided.
  2. Do not use "shall" in the AP when referencing what the contractor must provide.
  3. Do not use the term "Industry Partner," use "contractor."
  4. References to the incumbent contractor should be limited to appropriate sections of the AP, for example Contractual History.
  5. Make sure the AP is consistent, especially when discussing time frames, dollar amounts, and use of terms.
  6. The AP should be a self-contained document. Although the APM allows for attachments, the preference is to not have attachments unless absolutely necessary, with one exception; a full Market Research document should be included as an attachment to the AP.
  7. Tables in the Word version of the AP will transfer to APM without the border lines.
  8. Do not use symbols for a bulleted list; use either letters or numbers to allow for easy reference.

Additional Approvals Required:

FAR 16.207-3 requires approval by the "chief of the contracting office" (Chief Acquisition Officer (CAO) at GSA) for Firm-Fixed-Price (FFP), Level-of-Effort (LOE) term contracts exceeding $150,000.

Additional Guidance for maintaining AP:

APs should be reviewed and updated as necessary, but no less than annually or when there is a significant change (FAR 7.104(a)). The system of record, APM, does not support AP revisions after the AP is approved; therefore, AP updates must be documented in a memo to the file. Examples of significant changes requiring the AP to be updated include exercising an option or requesting a ceiling increase. Market research also needs to be conducted and the result should support 1) the requirement is still needed and 2) the price is fair and reasonable. See FAR Part 10.

Definitions:

FAR 2.101 defines bundling as:

(1) Consolidating two or more requirements for supplies or services, previously provided or performed under separate smaller contracts, into a solicitation for a single contract that is likely to be unsuitable for award to a small business concern due to-

(i) The diversity, size, or specialized nature of the elements of the performance specified;

(ii) The aggregate dollar value of the anticipated award;

(iii) The geographical dispersion of the contract performance sites; or

(iv) Any combination of the factors described in paragraphs (1)(i), (ii), and (iii) of this definition.

(2) "Separate smaller contract" as used in this definition, means a contract that has been performed by one or more small business concerns or that was suitable for award to one or more small business concerns.

(3) "Single contract" as used in this definition, includes-

(i) Multiple awards of indefinite-quantity contracts under a single solicitation for the same or similar supplies or services to two or more sources (see FAR 16.504(c)); and

(ii) An order placed against an indefinite quantity contract under a-

(A) Federal Supply Schedule contract; or
