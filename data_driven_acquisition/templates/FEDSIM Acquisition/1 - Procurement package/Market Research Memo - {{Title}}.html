Market Research Guidance and Template

 

What is market research?

Per the FAR Part 2 definitions, market research means collecting and analyzing information about capabilities within the market to satisfy agency needs. Market research is typically completed in two formats, strategic and tactical. For the purposes of this template, we are interested in tactical market research which is related to a specific procurement need, rather than an ongoing review of the industry in a specific sector.

 

Market Research Steps

●      Identify the Market

○      Standard industry terms and conditions for commercial items

○      Standard pricing/rates available for the government to utilize in developing the independent government cost estimate (IGCE)

○      To assess the number of available sources

○      To determine the sufficiency of small businesses to complete the requirement

●      Identify Sources of Information

○      Review current contract terms, prices, services and metrics

○      Review GWACS, MACs, and FSS contracts

○      Review market research links, technical literature and industry publications

○      Interview Agency experts and contracting officials re. Business issues, costs and performance

●      Collect Information

○      Search government databases: FPDS-NG, PPIRS, Acquisition Gateway

○      Issue Request for Information and narrow information needed from sources

○      Gather source list from GWACS, MACs and FSS websites

●      Document Results

○      Complete this form to comply with Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) Part 10.000, which describes the policies and procedures for conducting market research in order to arrive at the most suitable approach to acquiring, distributing, and supporting supplies and services. This part implements requirements of 41 U.S.C. 253a(a)(1), 41 U.S.C. 264b, 10 U.S.C. 2377, and 6 U.S.C. 796

 

When should I do market research?

FAR 10.002(b)(1) "The extent of market research will vary, depending on such factors as urgency, estimated dollar value, complexity, and past experience. The contracting officer may use market research conducted within 18 months before the award of any task or delivery order if the information is still current, accurate, and relevant."

 

Procurement Integrity:

In the process of conducting market research, it is possible that program office officials will need to have direct contact with industry representatives in order to gather the information needed for the procurement. While working with industry it is important to be aware of maintaining the integrity of the procurement.

 

Procurement integrity is set forth in FAR Part 3.104 which is based upon 41 U.S.C. chapter 21.

 

Vendors cannot assist in the development of requirements document if they intend to compete for the award.

 

As a safeguard, it is best to include a Contracting Officer in all discussions with industry.

 

If there is a failure to maintain the integrity of the procurement, there may be the following consequences:

●      Vendor may be precluded from competing in the procurement

●      Civil/Criminal penalties may be assessed against both the vendor and government employee involved depending on the level of violation.

DEFINITIONS OF TELECOMMUNICATIONS AND VIDEO SURVEILLANCE SERVICES OR EQUIPMENT (IAW NDAA SECTION 889)

 

"Covered telecommunications equipment or services", "Critical technology", and "Substantial or essential component" have the meanings provided in clause 52.204-25, Prohibition on Contracting for Certain Telecommunications and Video Surveillance Services or Equipment.

Prohibition. Section 889(a)(1)(A) of the John S. McCain National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2019 (Pub. L. 115-232) prohibits the head of an executive agency on or after August 13, 2019, from procuring or obtaining, or extending or renewing a contract to procure or obtain, any equipment, system, or service that uses covered telecommunications equipment or services as a substantial or essential component of any system, or as critical technology as part of any system. Contractors are not prohibited from providing -

(1) A service that connects to the facilities of a third-party, such as backhaul, roaming, or interconnection arrangements; or

(2) Telecommunications equipment that cannot route or redirect user data traffic or permit visibility into any user data or packets that such equipment transmits or otherwise handles.

 

The program office must include the following information:

 

(1) All covered telecommunications equipment and services offered (include brand; model number, such as original equipment manufacturer (OEM) number, manufacturer part number, or wholesaler number; and item description, as applicable);

 

(2) Explanation of the proposed use of covered telecommunications equipment and services and any factors relevant to determining if such use would be permissible under the prohibition in paragraph (b) of this provision;

 

(3) For services, the entity providing the covered telecommunications services (include entity name, unique entity identifier, and Commercial and Government Entity (CAGE) code, if known); and

 

(4) For equipment, the entity that produced the covered telecommunications equipment (include entity name, unique entity identifier, CAGE code, and whether the entity was the OEM or a distributor, if known).
